Nancy's Paper Folding Problem

The Problem

Nancy folded a rectangular piece of paper along the dotted line AB.

Before folding, the obtuse angle at the bottom is given as ∠x = 124°.

Our goal is to find the angle y step by step.

Step 1: Angles on a straight line

Look at the bottom edge of the paper at point B. A flat edge is a straight line, which equals 180°.

We can find the acute angle next to x by subtracting from 180°.

180° - 124° = 56°

Step 2: Alternate Interior Angles

Because the paper is a rectangle, the top and bottom edges are parallel.

The fold line AB acts as a diagonal line connecting them. This creates a "Z" shape.

Due to alternate interior angles, the angle at the top right is equal to the angle at the bottom left. They are both 56°.

Step 3: Folding the paper

Now, watch the paper fold along line AB.

When folded, the entire right side of the paper mirrors over the dotted line. The original lines are shown as dashed lines so you can see where the paper used to be.

Notice how our 56° angle moves from the right side of point A to the left side, overlapping the paper.

Step 4: The top straight line

Now let's look closely at the top edge of the paper at point A.

This straight line is 180°. It is now made up of three parts:

  • The angle y
  • The folded flap angle (56°)
  • The empty space where the paper used to be (56°)

Step 5: Calculate y

Since the three angles must add up to a straight line (180°), we can find y by subtracting the two 56° angles.

y + 56° + 56° = 180°

y = 180° - 112°

y = 68°

You have successfully solved for the missing angle!
